JANSUNWAI

"When the truth is spoken in front of all, lies have no place to hide. That is the strength of a Jan Sunwai"

Jansunwai, officially known as the Integrated Grievance Redressal System (IGRS), is an online portal and mobile application launched by the Uttar Pradesh (UP) government to facilitate the registration, tracking, and resolution of citizen grievances related to government services and departments. It aims to enhance transparency, accountability, and good governance through technology.

Launch:

Initiated in 2016 under Chief Minister Yogi Adityanath, it started at the district level and expanded to all governance levels, including state and central departments.

Purpose:

To provide a centralized, accessible platform for UP residents to lodge complaints, seek information, or raise demands about government schemes, public issues, or administrative inefficiencies, ensuring timely redressal and reducing corruption.

Meaning of the Term:

“Jansunwai” translates to “public hearing” in Hindi, reflecting its goal of listening to citizens’ concerns directly, akin to a virtual public forum.

Why is Jansunwai Needed?

  • Grievance Redressal: Citizens face issues like delayed pensions, land disputes, or non-delivery of government scheme benefits. Jansunwai ensures these are addressed systematically.
  • Transparency: By allowing online tracking and feedback, it reduces opaque processes and empowers citizens to hold officials accountable, complementing tools like RTI.
  • Anti-Corruption: The portal aims to curb malpractices in government work by enabling direct complaints against erring officials or departments.
  • Accessibility: It provides a 24/7 platform for filing complaints, eliminating the need for physical visits to government offices, which is especially beneficial for rural or migrant workers.
  • Good Governance: By streamlining communication between citizens and government, it aligns with UP’s goal of using technology for efficient administration.

Key Features of Jansunwai

  • Complaint Registration: Citizens can lodge complaints anytime, anywhere, via the portal or Samadhan mobile app. Registration requires a mobile number or email ID for OTP-based verification.
  • Tracking: Each complaint receives a 14-digit reference number sent via SMS/email, allowing users to track status online or via the app.
  • Reminders: If unresolved within time, reminders can be sent, monitored by higher authorities including the CM's Office.
  • Feedback: After resolution, users can rate quality; unsatisfactory cases may be reopened.
  • Notifications: SMS/email alerts inform users of updates or feedback requests.
  • Multi-Platform Integration: Complaints from all channels consolidated for departmental review.
  • Chief Minister Helpline (1076): 24/7 toll-free helpline from Lucknow, handling thousands of calls daily.
  • Anti-Land Mafia Portal: Dedicated space for illegal land encroachment complaints.

How Does Jansunwai Work?

  1. Register a Complaint: Go to jansunwai.up.nic.in or Samadhan app. Verify with OTP and submit complaint details.
  2. Track Status: Use reference number to view progress online or in the app.
  3. Send Reminders: If unresolved, escalate using the reminder option.
  4. Provide Feedback: Rate the resolution; poor scores may reopen cases.
  5. Alternative Channels: Use helpline 1076 or district grievance cells.

Relevance to Transparency and RTI

  • Synergy with RTI: Jansunwai complements RTI by reducing the need for formal requests through proactive grievance handling.
  • Transparency: Tracking, feedback, and auditing align with RTI’s goal of holding officials accountable.
  • Example: Citizens can file Jansunwai complaints about MGNREGA wage delays, and use RTI to access supporting documents if unresolved.

Challenges and Criticisms

  • Technical Glitches: Users report app crashes and data syncing issues.
  • Ineffective Redressal: Some cases closed without proper resolution.
  • Accessibility Barriers: Rural users with low digital access struggle to file or track complaints.
  • Overburdened System: High volume slows response times and increases case closures without due action.

Sentiment on X

  • Positive: Quick resolutions and accountability via feedback are appreciated.
  • Negative: Closure without resolution and department inaction frustrate users.

Benefits of Jansunwai

  • Available 24/7 via app/portal/helpline.
  • Enhances transparency and anti-corruption efforts.
  • Helps marginalized and migrant communities raise concerns.
  • Integrates multi-channel complaints into a single monitoring system.

Comparison with Other Systems

  • Bihar: lokshikayat.bihar.gov.in – similar digital portal for grievances.
  • Rajasthan: Samanvay Portal offers redressal services.
  • Jan Sunwai vs. Jansunwai: Traditional Jan Sunwai involves physical hearings (e.g., for MGNREGA social audits), while Jansunwai is fully digital.

Accessing Jansunwai

  • Portal: jansunwai.up.nic.in or samadhan.gov.in
  • App: Download from Google Play
  • Helpline: Dial 1076 (toll-free)
  • Official Documents: Available on the portal. RTI requests can also be filed for backend data.
  • Support: Email jansunwai-up@gov.in or jansunwaiup2016@gmail.com

How to Engage

  • Register a complaint via portal or app.
  • Track and escalate unresolved issues with reminders.
  • Use feedback mechanism for accountability.
  • File RTI if needed for follow-up documentation.
  • Call 1076 for urgent help.

Conclusion

Jansunwai (IGRS) is Uttar Pradesh’s digital grievance redressal system, launched in 2016 to address citizen complaints about government services, enhance transparency, and curb corruption. It allows 24/7 complaint registration, tracking, and feedback via jansunwai.up.nic.in, the Samadhan app, or helpline 1076, serving millions, including migrant workers. Features like unique reference numbers, SMS alerts, and social audits ensure accountability, complementing RTI’s role in governance. However, technical glitches, ineffective resolutions, and accessibility barriers persist, as noted on X. For official documentation, visit jansunwai.up.nic.in or contact UP’s Lok Shikayat Vibhag.
