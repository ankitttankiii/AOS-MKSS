MINIMUM INCOME GUARANTEE ACT

"A nation’s true progress is measured not by its billionaires, but by how it treats its poorest citizen"

The Rajasthan Minimum Guaranteed Income Bill, 2023, introduced by the Ashok Gehlot-led Congress government, is a landmark legislation in India designed to provide a legal entitlement to a minimum income for adult citizens in Rajasthan. Tabled on July 19, 2023, and passed on July 21, 2023, it builds on the framework of the Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (MGNREGA) while extending employment guarantees to urban areas and ensuring social security pensions for vulnerable groups. The Bill aims to address economic distress, reduce poverty, and promote social justice by guaranteeing income through employment and pensions. It is often described as a "revolutionary" step, though it has sparked debates about fiscal sustainability and political motivations.

Key Provisions of the Bill

The Bill covers three main areas:

  1. Right to Minimum Guaranteed Income:
    • Every adult citizen in Rajasthan is entitled to a minimum income for 125 days per year.
    • In rural areas: 100 days under MGNREGA + 25 days under Chief Minister Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme (CMREGS).
    • In urban areas: 125 days under Indira Gandhi Urban Employment Guarantee Scheme (IGUEGS).
    • Minimum wages paid weekly or within 15 days.
  2. Right to Guaranteed Employment:
    • Rural adults receive 125 days (100 MGNREGA + 25 CMREGS).
    • Urban adults receive 125 days under IGUEGS.
    • Unemployment allowance if work isn’t provided within 15 days of application.
  3. Right to Guaranteed Social Security Pension:
    • Eligible groups receive ₹1,000/month.
    • Pensions increase 15% annually to match inflation.

Objectives of the Bill

  • Poverty Alleviation
  • Employment Generation
  • Social Security
  • Economic Empowerment
  • Transparency and Accountability

Implementation Mechanism

  • Employment Schemes:
    • Rural: CMREGS + MGNREGA
    • Urban: IGUEGS (2022)
    • Job cards used to track wages/work
  • Pension Distribution:
    • Monthly DBT transfer to bank accounts
    • Beneficiaries identified through welfare databases
  • Funding:
    • ₹2,500 crore annually (est.)
    • Shared between state and central government
  • Monitoring and Transparency:
    • Social audits
    • RTI requests enabled
    • Use of platforms like NREGAsoft

Significance of the Bill

  • First law in India legally guaranteeing income
  • Extends employment guarantees to urban areas
  • Provides legal entitlement for pensions/employment
  • Focuses on social justice and RTI synergy

Relation to MGNREGA and RTI

  • MGNREGA:
    • Builds on 100-day guarantee
    • 125 days via CMREGS in rural areas
    • Job cards, payment timelines, and allowances follow MGNREGA model
  • RTI:
    • Ensures transparency via records access
    • Supports social audits and accountability

Criticisms and Challenges

  • Fiscal Sustainability: ₹2,500 crore annual burden; potential funding delays
  • Political Motivation: Labeled a "revdi" scheme ahead of elections
  • Implementation Challenges: Wage delays, weak urban framework, identifying beneficiaries
  • Scope Limitations: No universal pension/UBI; ₹1,000 may be inadequate

Comparison with Global Minimum Income Schemes

  • France – RSA: Income support for unemployed, with incentives to work
  • Italy – RdC: Conditional cash transfer with employment focus
  • Brazil – Bolsa Família: Requires schooling and health checkups
  • Rajasthan: Work-based guarantee and pension with legal enforceability

Sentiment on X

  • Supporters: Call it a game-changer and suggest scaling to 150 days
  • Critics: Label it populist and fiscally risky

How to Engage with the Bill

  • For Employment:
    • Register for job card (rural: Gram Panchayat, urban: ULB)
    • Apply under CMREGS or IGUEGS
    • Use RTI to check job/wage details
  • For Pensions:
    • Enroll via local authorities/social welfare office
    • Use RTI to verify pension disbursement
  • Monitoring:
    • Join social audits
    • File RTIs with Gram Panchayat or ULB PIO

Conclusion

The Rajasthan Minimum Guaranteed Income Bill, 2023, is a pioneering effort to ensure a minimum income through legally guaranteed employment (125 days annually) and social security pensions (₹1,000/month with 15% annual increases). By building on MGNREGA, extending to urban areas, and leveraging RTI for transparency, it addresses rural and urban poverty while empowering vulnerable groups. However, challenges like fiscal sustainability, implementation delays, and political controversies highlight the need for robust execution. Compared to global minimum income schemes, it stands out for its work-based approach and legal enforceability.
