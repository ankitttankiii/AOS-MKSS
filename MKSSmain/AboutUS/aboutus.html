About Us

"It is a people's organization in India that works for the rights of laborers and farmers, advocating for transparency and accountability in governance."

1. Historical Context and Formation

Despite democracy and welfare policies, rural communities in Rajasthan faced wage theft... MKSS was founded in 1990...

2. Ideology and Philosophy

  • Gandhian principles: Non-violence, swaraj, decentralization.
  • Ambedkarite thought: Constitutional rights, dignity, anti-caste framework.
  • Socialist values: Economic justice, fair wages, and anti-exploitation.

3. The Right to Information (RTI) Movement

MKSS launched a national movement for the Right to Information... public hearings...

4. Key Campaigns and Theoretical Significance

  • RTI as participatory democracy: Empowering citizens to ask questions.
  • Social Audits: Community-led inspections of government spending.
  • Minimum Wages Campaign: Using documents to demand legally mandated payments.

6. Achievements

  • Instrumental in the passage of the RTI Act (2005).
  • Established social audits in MGNREGA and other schemes.
  • Global recognition including the Ramon Magsaysay Award to Aruna Roy.

7. Impact and Legacy

  • Legal & political: Strengthened transparency laws and public accountability.
  • Academic: Cited in studies on governance, law, and public administration.
  • Global: Inspired RTI-style movements in other countries.
